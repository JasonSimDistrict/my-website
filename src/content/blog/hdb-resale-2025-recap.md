2025 was the year HDB resale stopped being a "ceiling" market and became a genuine supply-and-demand story. Million-dollar flats stopped making headlines because they stopped being unusual. Here's what actually happened — and what it means for 2026 buyers and sellers.

## The numbers

- **Median resale price up ~5.4% YoY.** A measured climb, not a runaway.
- **Million-dollar transactions: roughly 2.8% of all resale volume.** Up from 1.9% in 2024 — concentrated in mature estates and well-located 5-room/executive flats.
- **MOP supply increased by ~12%.** A wave of 2020-era BTOs hit the resale market as their MOP cleared.
- **Average days-on-market dropped to ~38 days.** Demand outpaced listing.

## What's driving demand

Three forces:

1. **Upgraders timing the move.** Many couples who got BTOs around 2020 are reaching the upgrade window — and a fair number are choosing to "upgrade in place" by selling and buying a larger resale rather than going private.
2. **Extended lease purchasers.** With lease-decay concerns easing in selected mature estates (thanks to VERS and SERS clarity), buyers are returning to flats with 70-80 years remaining.
3. **Cash-rich downsizers.** Right-sizers exiting larger private property for centrally-located resale with less maintenance overhead.

## The 2026 outlook

We expect:

- **Continued mid-single-digit price growth** — barring a cooling-measure intervention
- **MOP supply easing slightly** — fewer 2021-era BTOs reach MOP in 2026
- **Million-dollar share creeping up** — likely toward 3.5–4% by year-end
- **Increased policy attention** if growth accelerates — watch the August NDR for any signal

## What buyers should do

If you're buying resale in 2026:

- **Don't anchor on 2024 prices.** They're stale.
- **Move on the right unit, not the cheapest.** In a tight market, layout and location compound; PSF discounts on bad layouts don't.
- **Have your IPA ready before you offer.** Sellers in mature estates are seeing multiple offers and will favour buyers who can move fast.
