The 8 pillars PrimeKey evaluates

Each pillar is a proven growth factor that materially impacts a Singapore property's safety, liquidity, and long-term value. PrimeKey scores each one objectively, then rolls them up into a single 0–100% Investability Score.

01

MRT Connectivity

Walking distance to the nearest MRT — a proxy for demand and liveability across all 28 districts.

02

Growth Hotspots & Infrastructure

Proximity to URA growth zones with concentrated public and private infrastructure investment.

03

Government Land Sales

Upcoming GLS plots that historically lift surrounding resale values over a 5- to 7-year horizon.

04

Project Size & Liquidity

Larger projects offer richer amenities, scale efficiencies, and meaningfully higher resale liquidity.

05

Remaining Tenure

Lease length impacts financing, CPF usage, and resale value — particularly for sub-60-year remaining tenure.

06

Proximity to Primary Schools

Within-1km school catchments drive sustained family demand and demonstrable price premiums.

07

MOP Cluster & Upgrader Demand

Nearby HDB MOP flats signal future upgrader demand flowing into the surrounding private market.

08

Rental Demand & Yield

Yield bands by region — a defensive buffer in higher-rate environments and a forward indicator of rentability.

The grading score criteria

Once the 8 pillars are scored, PrimeKey rolls them up into a single health score — instantly visualised so you can compare any two projects on the same scale.

Green · > 75%
Investment Grade

Hard-to-fail asset across the 8 pillars. Sign your OTP with peace of mind — the downside is structurally protected.

Yellow · 50–75%
Moderate Risk

Mixed score profile. Worth a deeper look at the specific pillars that dragged the score down before committing.

Red · < 25%
Proceed with Caution

Material risks across multiple pillars. The upside has to be exceptional to justify the structural exposure.

Important to know

NAVIS PrimeKey is a research and shortlisting tool, not a valuation. It supplements (not replaces) property-specific considerations like layout, view, scarcity, and unit-level pricing. Use it alongside the 7 Steps Buyer Framework — particularly Steps 3, 4 and 5 — for the sharpest results.
