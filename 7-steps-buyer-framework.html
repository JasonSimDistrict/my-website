Self-Help Buyer Roadmap

The 7 Steps Buyer Framework for Singapore Property

A structured walkthrough designed to help you make confident decisions at every milestone — whether you're buying your first home or your fourth.

1

Define Your Why & Budget

Before browsing a single listing, get clear on intent and affordability. Owner-occupier and investor decisions look very different — and your budget isn't your loan ceiling.

  • Calculate your TDSR (Total Debt Servicing Ratio) cap of 55%
  • Apply MSR (Mortgage Servicing Ratio) 30% if buying HDB or EC
  • Account for BSD, ABSD, legal fees, and renovation buffer (~5–7% on top)
  • Stress-test your monthly payment against a 4% interest scenario
Get personal help with this step →
2

Understand HDB, EC, or Private

Each property class has its own eligibility rules, financing, and resale dynamics. Choosing the wrong category early is one of the costliest mistakes Singaporean buyers make.

  • HDB: BTO vs. resale, MOP, ethnic quotas, income ceilings
  • Executive Condo: 10-year MOP, 15-year privatisation, no more Deferred Payment Scheme (DPS) under the 2026 ruling
  • Private: freehold vs. 99-year, ABSD considerations for second properties
  • Decoupling vs. selling — when each makes sense
Discuss your scenario →
3

Shortlist by Location & Lifestyle

Don't fall for "hot district" lists — those are lagging indicators. Use a personal scoring framework that weighs your real life: commute, schools, family proximity, and future URA master plans.

  • Map your top-3 daily routes against MRT and bus connectivity
  • Check the URA Master Plan for upcoming amenities and rezoning
  • Score each shortlist on: lifestyle, growth potential, exit liquidity
  • Visit the area at three different times (morning, evening, weekend)
Get a shortlist review →
4

Evaluate Like an Investor

Even if you'll live in the home for 30 years, life happens. Buying with an exit-aware mindset protects your downside and maximises optionality.

  • Compare PSF against 5-year and 10-year district averages
  • Look at rental yield even if you won't rent — it's a signal of demand
  • Study unsold inventory and developer pricing power in the area
  • Identify two clear exit scenarios before signing the OTP
Run the numbers together →
5

Inspect & Compare: Showflats & Actual Units

New launch showflats are theatre, designed to inspire. Resale unit viewings are reality, designed to reveal. Different property types call for different inspection lenses — and the buyers who win are the ones who know which questions belong where.

  • Know what the differences are between showflats and resale units
  • Walk around the 500m radius for both types of property
  • Know what to take note of when viewing either type of property
  • Do comparison beyond the purchase price alone
Want a second opinion on a launch or resale unit? →
6

Secure Financing & OTP

The Option-to-Purchase clock moves fast. Have your financing in place before you commit — not after.

  • Get an In-Principle Approval (IPA) from at least two banks
  • Compare fixed vs. floating packages over 3-year and 5-year horizons
  • Understanding the OTP timeline: the difference between new launch and resale purchase
  • Engage a conveyancing lawyer early — don't wait for OTP signing
Need a financing comparison? →
7

Handover, Renovation & Beyond

The keys are just the start. The first 90 days set up the next 10 years of ownership cost and enjoyment.

  • Run a thorough defects inspection — document everything in writing
  • Understand DLP (Defects Liability Period) and how to claim
  • Plan renovation in phases; avoid over-customising for resale
  • Set a 3-year and 5-year review point to reassess your hold strategy
