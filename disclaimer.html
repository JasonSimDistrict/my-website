4. Property investment carries risk

All investments — including residential, commercial and overseas real estate — involve risk. Past performance of a district, project or asset class is not a reliable indicator of future results. Capital values, rental yields and resale liquidity can fall as well as rise, and may be affected by cooling measures, interest rate movements, supply pipelines, en-bloc cycles and macro conditions beyond anyone’s control. You should never commit to a purchase you cannot comfortably finance through a downturn.

5. Do your own due diligence

Readers are strongly encouraged to conduct their own due diligence before acting on anything published here. That means:

  • Confirming eligibility (HDB, EC, ABSD rates, citizenship rules) with HDB and IRAS directly.
  • Cross-checking PSF benchmarks against URA REALIS and developer price lists at the time of decision.
  • Reviewing the actual unit, stack, floor plan and view in person — not relying solely on showflat renderings.
  • Stress-testing affordability under MAS’s prevailing TDSR and MSR rules.
  • Reading the OTP, S&P and developer disclosures carefully with your appointed conveyancing lawyer.
